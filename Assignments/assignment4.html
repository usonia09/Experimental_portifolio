Proposition: The southern US states persist with the most stringent abortion policies

The chart below reveals a clear positive correlation between abortion service availability and the frequency of occurrences. By mapping the number of abortion providers to the incidence of abortions in 2017, this visualization offers valuable insights into their interconnected relationship.

FOR the Proposition

As highlighted earlier, a discernible positive correlation exists between the availability of abortion services and the incidence rates. This connection vividly underscores the restrictive nature of abortion policies in the southern states of the US, particularly evident in their record-low number of abortion services and the highest percentage of women living in counties without a clinic.

Moreover, the southern states further emphasize their stringent stance by consistently maintaining minimal to no financial contribution toward abortion services, with funding remaining absent from 2010 to 2015. This dual restriction—limited service availability and a lack of financial support—has led to a significant surge in the percentage of residents traveling out of the southern state to seek abortion services.

DECISIONS AND RATIONALES:

  1. Sorting in Ascending Order
    • Decision: Sorting the graph displaying the percentage of women living in countyies without a clinic in ascending order.
    • Rationale (Score: 2): the choice aimed to highlight the disparity in clinic availability across counties, particularly emphasizing the prevalence in the southern state.
  2. Averaging data
    • Decision: Averaging the data for all states.
    • Rationale (Score: 2): This decision was made earnestly, considering the absence of outliers that could distort the average. It reinforces the emphasis on the collective situation in the southern region without introducing misleading elements.
  3. Map title for Abortion expenditure
    • Decision: The word choice used in the title of the map representation of abortion expenditure
    • Rationale (Score: 1): The title choice was carefully crafted to direct the user's attention towards the minimal funds in the south and west.
  4. Line Graph and Color Choice for Resident Migration:
    • Decision: Choosing a line graph and utilizing a red color for illustrating the trend in the percentage of residents who moved states.
    • Rationale (Score: 2): The selection of a line graph effectively emphasized the positive trend in migration. The use of red added a dramatic effect without misleading.
  5. Arrangement of Visualizations to Tell a Story:
    • Decision: Intentional arrangement of individual visualizations to convey a narrative, emphasizing the lack of clinics, minimal expenditure, and subsequent migration.
    • Rationale (Score: 1): This choice was rated at 1, signifying that while truthful, it tells the reader how to read the visualization leaving not much room for self-interpretation.

AGAINST the Proposition

Since 2010, the southern states have made efforts to enhance accessibility to abortion services. This initiative began with an increase in funds allocated to abortion. States such as Texas, Alabama, Georgia, and North Carolina, which had previously allocated no funds towards abortion in 2010, demonstrated a notable shift by increasing their expenditure on abortion services by 2015.

This financial commitment was accompanied by a positive quadrupling of the change in abortion services. Specifically, the shift in abortion services from 2017 to 2020 was four times greater in the positive direction compared to the change recorded from 2014 to 2017. These adjustments are reflected in the increased rates of abortion in the southern states, positioning them with comparatively higher abortion rates.

DECISIONS AND RATIONALES:

  1. Color Code of the Map
    • Decision: Color coding the states that have funding for abortion services
    • Rationale (Score: -1): The color code was selected to convey a significant increase in funds allocated to abortion, creating a visual impact. However, the deception lies in the fact that the actual increase may not be as substantial. The decision to use color coding was preferred over bubble size, as the latter would reveal a more accurate representation of the minimal increase, contradicting the intended narrative.
  2. Comparison of State-Funded Abortion (2010) with Public Expenditure (2015)
    • Decision: comparing state-funded abortion in 2010 with public expenditure for abortion in 2015.
    • Rationale (Score: -2): The deception arises from the inclusion of federal funds in public expenditure, which may skew the perception of state commitment. This choice doesn't provide a clear distinction between state and federal contributions, leading to a misrepresentation .
  3. Capturing just The Below-zero Part of the Graph and Adding an Upward Arrow.
    • Decision: Capturing the below-zero part and adding upward arrow.
    • Rationale (Score: -2): This choice strategically zooms in on a slight rise in the change in abortion services in the south, diverting attention from the fact that overall values on the y-axis are in the negative range. The upward arrow serves to overemphasize the positive trend, drawing the viewer's attention even farther away from the y-axis leadings.
  4. Graph Showing Evolution in Change in Abortion Services (Southern States Only):
    • Decision: Refraining from including the visual representation of the progress made by other regions in the US
    • Rationale (Score: -2): The decision to exclusively showcase the southern states in this graph intentionally distorts the context. The southern states was very behind when compared to other regions thus not showing the other regions skewed the visualization to favor the desired narrative.
  5. Arrangement of Individual Visualizations:
    • Decision: The intentional arrangement of the individual visualizations
    • Rationale (Score: -2): the arrangement of the visualizations aimed at setting the plot for the reader to follow and thus deceptively guiding a reader to a conclusion that isn’t their own.

Final Reflection

In the process of crafting both visualizations, I discovered that a profound understanding of the data serves a dual purpose: it not only enables deceptive manipulation but also safeguards against unintentional deception. While immersed in the data, discrepancies between results and data references prompted a careful examination to discern if I misrepresented the data or if inaccuracies stemmed from the references. Once I achieved a precise understanding of the data, selecting the appropriate manipulation to shape the visualization's narrative became more straightforward.

In my revised perspective on ethical analysis and visualization, the emphasis shifted toward presenting the data as is, minimizing unnecessary interpretation for the reader. I recognized potential pitfalls in interpretations, such as emphasizing trends, selecting colors or wording, or excluding context. However, I acknowledge that each visualization carries a specific message, and removing such elements could risk losing that intended message. Additionally, providing readers complete freedom in their interpretations does not guarantee the most accurate understanding.

Consequently, I assert that ethical analysis involves transparently representing the data and providing essential context for a deeper comprehension. By presenting the data as is, accompanied by relevant context, ethical analysis promotes a more informed and responsible interpretation by the audience.
